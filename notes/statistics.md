### t score
* Tells how far a result is from another
* Can be used to tell how far live results are from simulated results
* How far results for one equity curve is from another
* Works well with smaller sample sizes
* https://www.statisticshowto.com/probability-and-statistics/t-distribution/t-score-formula/
* https://www.scribbr.com/frequently-asked-questions/what-is-a-t-score/
* https://www.indeed.com/career-advice/career-development/calculate-t-score
* z score vs t score: https://www.educba.com/z-score-vs-t-score/
### z score
* https://www.investopedia.com/terms/z/zscore.asp
* Could be used for instance to determine volatility of an asset compared to a mean (of say btc or all the assets combine). As such you could position size assets according to their z scores in order lower/raise exposure of the portfolio to volatility
