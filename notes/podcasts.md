### Derek Wong - How to think about strategies like a quant and diversify like a boss
https://podcasts.apple.com/jp/podcast/chat-with-traders/id957265404?l=en
* Your goal should be to reduce personal volatility, i.e. smooth our your returns across strategies by systemizing as much as you can, removing yourself as much as possible out of the equation so that your quirks and inadequacies don't show up in your pnl
* Each strategy has its own chart when you think about it. You can trade that chart as if the entire strategy is just one asset. So trade multiple strategies just as you would multiple assets, diversify and trade over the strategy level versus at the individual asset level. Imagine the abstraction of applying a trend following strategy not to a basket of assets but to a basket of strategies, jumping in the ones that are trending
* Along the lines of the previous point, diversification is paramount. Don't go for the basket of strategies that make you the most money but rather the ones that complement each other in such a way where they can mitigate each other's losses and even accentuate each other's gains
* Synthetic data. Just randomly generating a large price series and testing your trading strategy against that to begin with. Cuts out curve fitting and really nails down risk management before you get to the point of introducing the hidden quirks of a real price series. Something worth thinking about
